  • (1) Repeals the prior law (C.Cr.P. Art. 977(D)) limitation on obtaining an expungement once every five years for an arrest and conviction of a misdemeanor offense and once every 10 years for a misdemeanor DWI offense. Act 78
  • (2) Repeals the prior law (C.Cr.P. Art. 978(D)) limitation on obtaining an expungement once every 15 years for an arrest and conviction of a felony offense. Act 78
  • New law amends prior law to limit the prohibition on filing a motion for an expungement of a record of arrest to only those persons who are in the physical custody of the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections and serving a sentence at hard labor. Act 78

  • Act. 342 LA Code Crim Pro Art. 977(D) (2023)
  • LA Code Crim Pro Art. 977(D) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a person may file a motion to expunge his record of arrest and conviction of a misdemeanor conviction for a first offense possession of marijuana, tetrahydrocannabinol, or chemical derivatives thereof after ninety days from the date of conviction.

  • Art. 893. Suspension and deferral of sentence and probation in felony cases LA Code Crim Pro Art. 893 (2022)
  • LA Code Crim Pro Art. 893(E)(2) Upon motion of the defendant, if the court finds at the conclusion of the probationary period that the probation of the defendant has been satisfactory, the court may set the conviction aside and dismiss the prosecution. The dismissal of the prosecution shall have the same effect as acquittal, except that the conviction may be considered as a first offense and provide the basis for subsequent prosecution of the party as a habitual offender except as provided in R.S. 15:529.1(C)(3). The conviction may be considered as a prior offense for purposes of any other law or laws relating to cumulation of offenses. Dismissal under this Paragraph shall occur only twice with respect to any person.
  • After a client successfully files an 893 Motion the conviction is then eligible for expungement. If the conviction was already eligible for expungement an example of when it may still be beneficial to them to first file an 893 Motion is if they are seeking employment in a field that is able to see expunged records. There is no charge to file an 893 Motion.

  • Art. 894. Suspension and deferral of sentence; probation in misdemeanor cases LA Code Crim Pro Art. 894 (2022)
  • La Code Crim Pro Art. 894(B)(1) When the imposition of sentence has been deferred by the court, as authorized by this Article, and the court finds at the conclusion of the period of deferral that the defendant has not been convicted of any other offense during the period of the deferred sentence, and that no criminal charge is pending against him, the court may set the conviction aside and dismiss the prosecution; La Code Crim Pro Art. 894(B)(2) The dismissal of the prosecution shall have the same effect as an acquittal
  • After a client successfully files an 894 Motion the conviction is then eligible for expungement. If the conviction was already eligible for expungement an example of when it may still be beneficial to them to first file an 894 Motion is if they are seeking employment in a field that is able to see expunged records. There is no charge to file an 894 Motion.
